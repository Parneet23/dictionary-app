Apple A round fruit with red, yellow, or green skin and a crisp, white flesh. Apples are commonly eaten raw or used in various culinary dishes, desserts, and beverages.
Banana A long curved fruit with a yellow skin and soft, sweet flesh. Bananas are a good source of potassium and are often eaten raw or used in smoothies, desserts, and baking.
Orange A citrus fruit with a bright orange skin and juicy, segmented flesh. Oranges are known for their sweet and tangy flavor and are commonly eaten fresh or juiced.
Strawberry A small, red fruit with seeds on the surface and a sweet, juicy flesh. Strawberries are often eaten fresh, used in desserts, or made into jams and preserves.
Grapes Small, round or oval-shaped fruits that grow in clusters on vines. Grapes come in various colors, including green, red, and purple, and are commonly eaten fresh or used to make wine, juice, and raisins.
Pineapple A tropical fruit with a tough, spiky exterior and sweet, juicy yellow flesh. Pineapples are often eaten fresh or used in fruit salads, desserts, and savory dishes.
Watermelon A large, juicy fruit with a thick green rind and sweet, red flesh dotted with black seeds. Watermelons are commonly enjoyed fresh in slices or cubes, especially during hot summer months.
Mango A tropical fruit with a smooth, colorful skin and sweet, juicy flesh. Mangos are often eaten fresh, blended into smoothies, or used in salads, salsas, and desserts.
Kiwi A small, oval-shaped fruit with brown fuzzy skin and bright green flesh speckled with tiny black seeds. Kiwis have a tangy-sweet flavor and are commonly eaten fresh or used in fruit salads and desserts.
Blueberry Small, round berries with a deep blue-purple color and a sweet, tart flavor. Blueberries are often eaten fresh, used in baking, or added to cereals, yogurt, and smoothies.
